Heading Tags in HTML:

HTML provides six levels of heading tags, used to define headings and subheadings within a webpage, creating a hierarchical structure for content. These tags range from <h1> to <h6>

<h1>: Represents the highest level of heading, typically used for the main title of a page or section. It usually displays the largest and boldest text by default.
<h2>: Represents a subheading, a sub-section of the <h1> content.
<h3>: Represents a sub-subheading, a sub-section of the <h2> content.
<h1>,<h5>,<h6>: Represent further levels of subheadings, with <h6> being the lowest and usually displaying the smallest text.
